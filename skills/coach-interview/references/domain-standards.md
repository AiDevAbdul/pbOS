# Coach Interview — domain standards

## Coaching posture
- **Listen more than you ask.** The goal is to understand a human, not fill a form.
- **One question at a time.** Batching produces shallow answers. Record each before the next.
- **Probe thin answers once.** "Say more about that" beats moving on.
- **Triangulate.** People misjudge their own strengths and are blind to their gaps. Self-report is a hypothesis; the person's existing content is the evidence.

## The 8 layers (each has a distinct job — don't skip)
1. **context_intent** — who/what/why-now + 12-month success. → archetype signal.
2. **identity** — origin story, defining moments, contrarian POV, zone of genius, values. (The richest fuel for positioning.)
3. **strengths** — what they're known for, *with proof* for each claim.
4. **weaknesses** — avoidances, fears, what's held them back, constraints. (Threats woven in.)
5. **audience** — who they reach, from-what-to-what transformation.
6. **competitive** — peers/admired, category-of-one. (Saturation threats woven in.)
7. **capacity** — sustainable hours, writer vs talker vs on-camera. → platform + cadence.
8. **voice** — samples (or live transcript) + banned words.

## Goal archetypes (detect at layer 1, confirm aloud)
| Archetype | Brand exists to… | Branch emphasis |
|---|---|---|
| `consultant_leadgen` | win inbound clients | offer + ideal-client trigger |
| `founder_thought_leader` | unlock funding/hiring/distribution | category to own |
| `creator_monetization` | grow audience → revenue | monetization path + platform fit |
| `career_capital` | reputation + opportunities | whose attention + target roles |

Detection (`detectArchetype`) is a deterministic keyword rubric over the layer-1 answers — a starting point, **never a verdict**. Confirm with the person before branching.

## Triangulation rule
A claimed strength becomes a SWOT **strength** only if it shares a meaningful keyword with the topics surfaced from `voice_samples/` (`audit-evidence`). Otherwise it's logged as a **blind spot** in weaknesses ("Claimed but unverified…"). No evidence ⇒ flag lower confidence; don't fabricate corroboration.

## SWOT mapping (the engine does this deterministically)
- **Strengths** ← evidence-corroborated claimed strengths.
- **Weaknesses** ← self-reported weaknesses + unverified claims.
- **Opportunities** ← audience gaps ("no one addresses X well").
- **Threats** ← competitor saturation + the person's constraints.
