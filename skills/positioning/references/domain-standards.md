# Positioning — domain standards

## The positioning statement (internal anchor, NOT a public slogan)
Use this 5-part formula:

> For **[specific audience]** who **[struggle / want]**, **[person]** is the **[category / frame]** that **[transformation]**, because **[reason to believe — drawn from identity/credentials]**.

Example:
> "For early-stage CTOs drowning in scaling chaos, I turn complex systems into calm, teachable clarity — because I spent 10 years building them."

## The four positioning fields
- **niche** — the narrow space they own. Narrow beats broad; "for everyone" is for no one.
- **audience** — a specific person, not a demographic blob.
- **transformation** — from-what → to-what. The value is the change, not the topic.
- **category_of_one** — the honest, evidence-backed reason they're not interchangeable with peers (pull from the discovery `contrarian_pov`, `zone_of_genius`, and competitive layer).

## Rules
- **Never blank-page.** Synthesize strictly from the discovery layer (identity, SWOT, audience).
- **Sharpen, don't inflate.** The category-of-one must trace to real strengths/credentials — anything else fails the authenticity guard downstream.
- **Human owns the call.** Present the statement; the person approves; only then `--approve-positioning`.

## Good vs bad
- ❌ "I help people grow on social media." (no niche, no transformation, no edge)
- ✅ "I help bootstrapped SaaS founders turn churn into a referral engine — the retention-first growth voice in a space obsessed with acquisition."
